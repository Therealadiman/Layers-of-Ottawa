Layers of Ottawa

An Augmented Reality Retelling of the City's History

Purpose of the Website

This website was created to introduce users to the "Layers of Ottawa" augmented reality experience, by offering a central hub where users can understand the project, explore visuals, and learn how to interact with the AR gallery.

The goal of this project is to make history more engaging by combining storytelling with immersive augmented reality technology, allowing users to experience Ottawa’s past in an interactive way.

Uses of the Website

  • Provides instructions for accessing the AR experience
  • Displays visuals such as the vision board and screenshots
  • Explains the concept of the project
  • Educates users about Ottawa’s history
  • Acts as a presentation tool for the project

About Ottawa’s History

Ottawa’s history begins with Indigenous peoples, especially the Algonquin Anishinaabeg, who used the Ottawa River as a major trade route.

The city later grew through the construction of the Rideau Canal (1826–1832) and the lumber trade. In 1857, Queen Victoria selected Ottawa as the capital of Canada, transforming it into a political centre.
